Classic Irish Lamb Stew

Description

Savor the rich tradition of Irish Lamb Stew, a culinary masterpiece that captures the essence of Ireland in every hearty bite. Tender lamb, slow-cooked with wholesome vegetables, melds in a symphony of flavors, creating a soul-warming dish that pays homage to Ireland's culinary heritage. Immerse yourself in the comforting aroma and robust taste of this iconic Irish fare, a celebration of simple ingredients coming together to create a timeless, delicious experience. Discover the heart and soul of Ireland through the warmth of a bowl of Irish Lamb Stew, where tradition meets culinary excellence.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 pounds boneless lamb shoulder, cut into 1 ½ inch pieces
  • ½ teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and cut into large chunks
  • 1 parsnip, peeled and cut into large chunks (Optional)
  • 4 cups water, or as needed
  • 3 large potatoes, peeled and quartered
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh rosemary (Optional)
  • 1 cup coarsely chopped leeks
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a large stockpot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
  2. Add lamb pieces and cook, stirring gently, until evenly browned. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Stir in onion, carrots, and parsnips and cook gently for a few minutes.
  4. Stir in water. Cover and bring to a boil; reduce heat to low and simmer until lamb is tender, about 45 to 1 hour, depending on the cut of meat you used.
  5. Stir in potatoes, and simmer for 15 to 20 minutes, before adding leeks and rosemary.
  6. Continue to simmer uncovered, until potatoes are tender but still whole.
  7. Serve piping hot in bowls garnished with fresh parsley.
  8. Enjoy!
